Past Events

Here are the events/meetups that we organized in the past


Meetup #5: Cloud Study Jam (AI/ML)
Saturday, Mar 2, 2019 | 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM

AI and ML focused cloud study jam with deep dive sessions around Big Query, Cloud SQL, NLP, ML and Cloud Speech. A good enthusiastic crowd that saw engaging discussions and interactive sessions which was well received by the attendees.


Meetup #4: BigData & Analytics
Friday, Dec 7, 2018 | 3:30 PM to 6:30 PM

The theme this time was bigdata and analytics and what better place to have it hosted than a world-class analytics company in Bridgei2i. Excellent topics around Elasticache, Clickstream analytics and creation of data lakes were discussed all with a flavor of GCP blended in.


Meetup #3: GCDCBLR + Serverless Architects
Saturday, September 8, 2018 | 9:30 AM to 1:00 PM

Another edition of Cloud Native event was conducted as a joint meetup with Serverless Architects group. Participants got to hear insights from a container expert at IBM on upcoming workload isolation and encryption technologies followed by a session on running C++ code in a serverless manner with Cloud Functions.


Meetup #2: Google Cloud Next’18 Extended
Tuesday, July 24, 2018 | 8:00 PM to 11:00 PM

Google Cloud Next’18 is the annual developer festival at Google and we hosted one here in Bangalore at the Google office as an Extended event! The attendees got to hear from Google Cloud leaders about how the cloud is transforming business, making AI available to everyone and improving the lives and circumstances of people around the world in ways never before possible.


Meetup #1: Containers/K8S/Serverless
Saturday, July 14, 2018 | 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM

Held as a joint meetup with Cloud Native and Container Developers group. The event was focused on serverless with a talk on Cloud Functions followed by hands-on K8S lab. Target audience were beginners, so just a basic understanding of containers were the only prerequisite. There was also a special talk from Hasura about their GraphQL Engine.

  • Community guidelines open_in_new
    • Be nice - We're all part of the same community, so be friendly, welcoming, and generally a nice person.

    Be respectful and constructive - Don't get into flamewars, make personal attacks, vent or rant unconstructively.

    Be collaborative - Work together! We can learn a lot from each other.

    Participate - Join in on discussions, show up for in-person meetings regularly, offer feedback, and help implement that feedback.

    Step down considerately - If you know that a new job or personal situation will limit your time, find someone who can take over for you and transfer the relevant information (contacts, passwords, etc.) for a smooth transition.

    Basic etiquette for online discussions - Don’t send messages to a big list that only need to go to one person. Keep off topic conversations to a minimum.
    Close
  • Organizer acknowledgements open_in_new
    • 1. We abide by the Google Event Community Guidelines and Anti- Harassment Policy

    2. It’s NOT ok to use GCDC for profit. Organizers should only charge attendees for ticket entry and/or get sponsorships to cover costs of event operations (e.g. food and drinks, venue, setup, speakers) if needed.

    3. It’s ok to partner with other groups and companies to arrange speakers, venues, and sponsorships.

    4. It’s ok to talk about non-Google technologies in your community. We want to promote learning across technologies without bashing any company including Google or others.

    5. The use of the GCDC name/logo is granted to organizers so long as they are in good standing with Google Developers and follow the brand guidelines.
